Mohit and Abby’s Reception

So what’s going on, huh?

Hey! Hi! Hello!

We hope you’re all doing well! First things first, thank you for manually typing in a full URL to be with us right now in this virtual way. Abby doesn’t believe in QR codes, which means we all must unfortunately and unnecessarily sacrifice an extra 45 seconds of our time to find this web page.

We’re anticipating that some people may be confused as to what this event actually is, so hopefully this can clarify what’s happening. Abby and Mohit very recently got engaged in a super normal way, but now they have to get married in a super un-normal way. Basically, it’s not just Abby and Mohit calling the shots because there’s a top-secret third party involved with just as much (way more) authority on this timeline as them: the U.S. government.

They are currently in the process of waiting for their K-1 visa (colloquially and fittingly referred to as the ‘Fiancé(e) visa’) to be approved. This visa allows Mohit to enter the U.S. with the specific intention of getting married to Abby. Therefore, they must wait for this application to be approved before they are allowed to get married. The process takes an unknown and unpredictable amount of time (usually about a year). Once it is approved, Mohit will have another prescribed amount of time to enter the country and then they will have 90 days to get married (just like the TLC show).

As romantic as that sounds, they don’t want to plan a wedding in 90 days. This event is happening in lieu of a formal wedding or a formal wedding reception (so you won’t be randomly getting an invite to another one of these in 8 to 12 months). Once their visa is approved, the two of them are planning to elope somewhere and there will be no further celebrations. Meaning! This is all you get, folks.

But like they say, good things come to those who wait! This process means they can hopefully move to the U.S. together in the coming years and then all you proud to be Americans will be graced with their presence till death do us part! Mwah!
